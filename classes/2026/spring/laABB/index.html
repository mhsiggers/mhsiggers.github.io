Syllabus

This course introduces linear algebra as the core mathematical language of modern engineering, computer science, and data-intensive applications. The course emphasizes geometric insight, computation, algorithms, and hands-on applications. Students learn how matrices, vectors, and linear transformations underpin machine learning models, computer vision pipelines, dimensionality reduction techniques, network analysis, cryptographic systems, and large-scale optimization. We work from classnotes, the main reference for which is Gilbert Strang's "Linear Algebra and Learning from Data"; we refer to other sources by Strang for further detail. The course is organized around four themes: Matrices and Linear Systems — fast computational tools for solving Ax = b and least squares. Vector Spaces and Bases — understanding data, signals, and models through coordinates. Orthogonality and SVD — the theoretical backbone of PCA, data compression, and low-rank models. Eigenvalues and Applications — stability, Markov chains, PageRank, and spectral methods. The actual schedule of material will depend on the background of the the students, but the following is an idealised schedule based on "Linear Algebra and Learning from Data" that we would try for in the case that the students have a basic background in linear algebra.
