Syllabus

This is a standard undergraduate introduction to abstract algebra. Abstract algebra is a basic subject in mathematics in which we look to develop the notions we are familiar with from various concrete arithematical settings, in a generic setting based on only minimal axioms. Taking up from where we left off in Algebra I, looking at groups, we move to looking at rings and fields.

We plan to cover Chapters 12 - 22 of the text. Here is a tentative weekly schedule.
