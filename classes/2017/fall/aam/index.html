Syllabus

In this course we look at the application of the probabilistic method to combinatorial problems. The 'probabilistic method' is the use of non-deterministic, or random, constructions to prove the existence of structures with certian properties. Applying the probabilistic method will require a us to become familiar enough with the basics of probability theory, to use certain commons tools. Our applications will take us through a nice smattering of classical graph and combinatorial problems. The course requires little as a prerequisite but a certain level of mathematical maturity.

We will follow the notes The Probabilistic Method -- Lecture Notes by Matoucek and Vondrak, which you should be able to find online. These notes are based on the book The Probabilistic Method (2nd edition) by Alon and Spencer. At places we will refer also to this book, and we will use it for problems.
