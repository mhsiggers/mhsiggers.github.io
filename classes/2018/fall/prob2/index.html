Prerequisites

I expect that you have covered most of Chapters 1 through 4 of the clas text, or similar material.
  • You should have an understanding of probability spaces and discrete and continuous random variables; and should be able to compute basic expectations such as mean varience and moment generating functions.
  • You should know how to transform multivariate distributions and find their marginal and conditional distributions.
  • You should have seen basic distributions and in particular, be quite familiar with binomial, poisson and normal distributions.
  • You should understand what a random experiment is and be able to compute confidence intervals and set up a hypothesis test.

Syllabus

We will cover the main topics from Chapters 5 - 9 of the class text. Depending on the classes familiarity with the prerequisites, and the rigour with which you leared them, we may spend the first week or two reviewing them.

The main topics this semester are an analysis of various measures of 'goodness' of estimators.
