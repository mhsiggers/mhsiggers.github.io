Syllabus

This class builds background and techniques for students hoping to do research in the area of graph homomorphisms. We develop the theory of universal algebra as it applies to the problem of graph homomorphisms, databases, and constraint satisfaction problems. In the first half of the class we will overview the basic notions of computational complexity, graph homomorphisms and constraint satisfication, and applied universal algebra, and the CSP dichotomy. In the second half of the class we will work from research papers, digging into the details on specific topics. The following is suggestive of the topics we will cover:
  • Hell and Nesetril: The H-colouring dichotomy
  • Feder and Vardi: The CSP Dichotomy Conjecture
  • Bulatov Jeavons and Krohkin: The Classification Conjecture
  • Basics to Birkoff: A closer look at univesal algebra
  • Maroti-MacKenzie: Mal'cev classifications
  • Bulatov, Barto-Kozik-Niven, more Bulatov, and Bulatov and Zhuk: Steps to the Dichotomy
  • Midterm
  • Larose Loten Tardif Zadori: Near Unanimity graphs
  • Feder Hell Huang Rafiey: List-Hom on graphs
  • MacGillvray and Wiles: Tournaments
  • Larose and Tardif: Getting topological

Grading

Grading will be based on presentations and assignments, and possibly (depending on the performance on assignments) inclass tests. To get a C, a student should have reasonable attendence. To get a B, a student should be able to properly state definitions and main results. ('Properly' means in a logically consistant way; English need not be perfect. ) For a low A, a student should be able to reproduce, the shorter proofs and arguments from class. For a high A, a student should be able to apply results and arguments in class to unseen problems.
