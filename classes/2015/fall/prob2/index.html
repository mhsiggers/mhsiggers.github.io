Syllabus

Over two semester's we will try to cover chapters 1 through 9 of the text. In the first semester we will covered most of chapters 1 through 4.

The main topics this semester are an analysis of various measures of 'goodness' of estimators.
Here is an approximate schedule.

Week Sections Topics
1 5.1 Convergence in Probability
2 5.2 Convergence in Distribution
3 5.3, 6.1 Central Limit Theorem, Maximum Likelihood Estimates
4 6.2 - 6.3 Efficiency, Likelihood Tests
5 7.1- 7.3 Sufficiency
6 7.3 - 7.4 Completeness and Uniqueness
7 - Test week 1
8 7.5 - 7.6 The Exponential Class, Functions of a Parameter
9 7.7 - 7.9 More Parameters and Independence
10 8.1 - 8.2 Most Powerful Tests
11 8.2 - 8.3 Likelihood Ratio Tests
12 8.4 - 9.1 Sequential Probability, Quadratic Forms
13 9.2, 9.5 Analysis of Variance
14 - Test week 2

Class Format

We will discuss the format of the class in the first week.

Depending on the size of the class, (with some deference to class opinion) the format will be one of
  • standard lecture format, with no homework but two tests for evaluation,
  • student lectures, evaluation being based on lectures,
  • some combination of the above.

Homework

There will be homework problems assigned for each class. You don't have to do them, but the test, if we have it, will draw largely from them.

Attendence

Attendence will be casually monitored, but with devestating consequences if attendence becomes too casual.
