Syllabus

This course is a 'Writing for Mathematics' course. We will focus on technical writing-- learning and practising how to clearly describe processes and to layout arguments. We will look at descriptive technical writing, in general, but will concentrate on those practices that are specific to mathematics. We will also introduce, and become familiar with LaTeX, the software that is used to typeset mathematics, and spend time learning to use it properly.

Classes will consist of short lectures, followed by in class writing assignments that will be discussed and edited for the class. There will also be longer weekly writing assignments that will be handed in.

The course is about writing mathematics, and to write mathematics one must understand it. (Or one must be able to understand it, often it is only in writing it that one truely comes to understand it.) So students should have some mathematical background. However, the subject of the writings, as much as possible, will be adjusted to the individual student's mathematical ability.

Weekly Assignments

There will be weekly writing assignments, sometimes based on readings. These will be handed in for comment, but not for evaluation, and should then be corrected and handed in again. Points will be given for timely submission.

Midterm and Final Essays

There will be two longer writing assignments that will be handed it for evaluation. The first of these will be one page, and due during the mid-term week, the second will be two pages and will be due in the final exam week.

Grading

Possibly we will have a mix of foreign and Korean students in this class. Though our focus is 'writing for mathematics', ability in English will give students an advantage. Grading must be relative, but foreign students will be removed from the grade limits, so effectivly Korean students are only evaluated relative to Korean students. Foreign students will be graded relative to their English abilitiy, whatever that means.
