Syllabus

The objective of the class is to see a broad range of basic topics of classical graph theory. We will cover topics with mathematical rigour and proof, but only to an extent. We opt for easier proofs and algorithmic results over deeper proofs making the course something of an introduction to proof for those students who have a less mathematical background. We will spend about a week on each of the following topics.
  • Basics, Degree Sequences
  • Eulerian and Hamiltonian Graphs
  • Trees
  • Connectivity and Menger's Theorem
  • Planarity and Kuratowski's Theorem
  • Vertex colouring, Brooks' Theorem
  • 4-colour problem, List colouring, Thomassen's Five-list-colouring theorem.
  • Matchings, Hall's Theorem
  • Graph Homomorphisms
  • Perfect Graphs
  • Extremal Graph Theory, Turan's Theorem
  • Ramsey Theory
  • The probabilistic Method
