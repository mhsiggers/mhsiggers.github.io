We will use the above text as a main resource to learn about the field of Parameterised Complexity.

Parameterised Complexity

In the area of complexity. In our usual setting we try to show that an NP problem is P (polynomial time solvable) or is NP-complete. In this course we introduce graph parameters k (such as tree-width) and look at what happens when we fix the parameter. We see such complexity classes as FPT (fixed parameter tractable) and XP (slice-wise polynomial) and try to decide if the problem is in one of these classes, or is complete with respect to (assumed) bigger classes.

Chapter 1 of the text gives a fairly readable flavour of what the course is about. We will cover this, but it maybe would give you a feel about if the course is interesting to you.

How the seminar runs

My grad students and I will start off presenting sections of the text, and any necessary background that the text does not give beyond our usual setting of P and NP.

Other grad students and undergrad students are welcome to attend, and we will make reasonable efforts to fill in background, but we will not spend a lot of time reviewing basic graph notions, basic P vs. NP complexity, graph Homomorphism, and CSP. Basic graph notions can be found anywhere. For the other things, these Homomorphism class notes give a concise, but not flawless, introduction.

Other students will be welcome to present sections if they would like to, and we would appreciate it, but there is not expectation that you will.

Depending on how things go, we may move from the text to presenting related research papers.
